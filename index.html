Well Intervention Catalog

Challenge: The sales team lacked cohesive marketing materials to support training, customer engagement, and promotion of new technologies.

Goals: Create a comprehensive catalog system to serve as a training tool, customer reference, and scalable marketing asset.

Strategy: Develop a unified content and design system that could clearly communicate technical information, elevate visual consistency, and support global sales enablement.

Execution: Over the course of 10 months, I led the end-to-end creative direction and project management of a cross-functional team of eight—including two copywriters, three designers, a photographer, and an illustrator. I oversaw content development, coordinated team workflows, and ensured visual alignment with brand standards. We produced a three-book series: product sheets, field case studies, and technical papers—supported by custom illustrations, new photography, and redrawn graphics for improved clarity. The collection was packaged in a custom-designed box and accompanied by a signed executive message for premium delivery.

Branding: Maintained corporate brand compliance while elevating the visual system for usability and impact. Applied clean layouts and intuitive navigation to enhance customer readability and global sales utility.

Deliverables: 92 product sheets, 78 case studies, technical documentation, photography, custom illustrations, and packaging design—delivered across international markets.

Results: Successfully expanded the segment’s marketing library, increased brand visibility internally and externally, and enabled the distribution of over 1,000 catalogs from the initial 1,500 print run to sales teams and key global customers.

Six Ways to Reduce Well Intervention Costs

Challenge: Traditional brochures and product sheets lacked the impact needed to elevate this product family. The objective was to create a high-value asset that could perform across print, digital, email, and web—while communicating technical depth and measurable results.

Goals: Position the portfolio as a proven solution for reducing intervention costs, using real case studies and operator outcomes—packaged in a format flexible enough for executive briefings, sales tools, and mass distribution.

Strategy: As Art Director and Project Lead, I guided the creative and production process from concept through execution. The goal: move beyond templated layouts and build a visually compelling, benefit-focused narrative centered on operational value and savings.

Execution: I oversaw content structure, visual hierarchy, and cross-functional coordination across design, copywriting, and technical teams. The final deliverable included a print insert for the Journal of Petroleum Technology (JPT), an interactive digital PDF, and supporting assets for email and web. The layout segmented each tool by benefit theme, supported by custom 3D renderings, stylized iconography, and concise messaging to make complex content easily digestible.

Branding: Delivered a fresh, clean design that broke away from legacy templates while maintaining brand integrity. Visual storytelling and technical clarity ensured the piece performed across internal, customer-facing, and editorial platforms.

Deliverables: JPT magazine insert, digital PDF with embedded links, email campaign assets, product spotlight graphics, and web content integration.

Results: Published in JPT’s June 2016 issue (75,000+ circulation), the piece drove significant product page traffic, multiple customer inquiries, and 14,512 digital downloads through the spotlight feature.
